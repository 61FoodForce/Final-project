Food Waste

In the United States, more than one third of available food gets thrown away (USDA). The food that we throw away ends up in landfills, where it decomposes and creates large amounts of a gas called methane. Like carbon dioxide, methane is considered a green house gas due to how easily it can absorbs the sun's heat, which contributes to climate change.

Hunger in America



As many as 42 million Americans are experiencing hunger today, with children making up almost a fourth of that statistic (Feeding America). A lack of a consistent source of food during childhood can cause cognitive defects and other long term health issues.
